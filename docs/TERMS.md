## 2. Accounts and roles

Klink workspaces have defined roles, each with specific permissions enforced by the system:

- **Pastor** (one per church) — full oversight; the only role that can approve/delete fundraising projects
- **Elder** (up to 25) — the only role that can appoint a new Pastor or delete the church
- **Manager** (up to 10) — operations and content; can demote a Pastor/Elder as a checks-and-balances safeguard
- **Financial Secretary** — records church money; cannot post content or manage members
- **Group Admin / Group Financial Secretary** — manage an individual ministry group only
- **Member** — the base role for anyone who joins with a church's join code

Role limits, permissions, and restrictions (e.g. a Manager cannot touch money; a Financial Secretary cannot manage members) are enforced by the platform and are part of how Klink keeps church operations accountable. Users agree not to attempt to circumvent these role restrictions.

## 3. Church workspace registration

- A church registers with a single form and receives a private, isolated workspace and a join code.
- The registering Pastor is responsible for the accuracy of the church's information and for appointing trustworthy Elders and Managers.
- Each church's data is completely isolated from every other church on the platform.

## 4. Member registration

- Members join using their church's join code and register with a phone number or email.
- Verification codes are sent by SMS (Twilio) or email (Gmail) to confirm identity.
- Members without smartphones can be registered by a leader and reached via SMS.
- Users must provide accurate registration information and are responsible for keeping their login credentials secure.

## 5. Payments and giving

- Online giving (tithe, offering, welfare, building, missions, special, project contributions) is processed through **Paystack**, using a hosted payment page that accepts card or Mobile Money.
- Klink verifies every transaction server-side before recording it, and each transaction is recorded exactly once.
- **Klink does not store card details.** Payment card data is handled entirely by Paystack's hosted page.
- Refunds, chargebacks, and payment disputes are subject to Paystack's terms and the policies of the member's card issuer or Mobile Money provider — Klink facilitates the transaction record but is not a payment processor itself.
- Welfare contributions must be exact multiples of the church-configured monthly amount; the system enforces this automatically.
- Offline/cash contributions may be recorded on a member's behalf by the Financial Secretary.

Leaders are responsible for the content they post on behalf of their church (announcements, events, sermons, devotionals, polls). Polls, once created, are immutable — leaders should review poll questions and options carefully before publishing, as they cannot be edited afterward.

## 7. Prayer requests

Prayer requests may be submitted publicly or privately. Private requests are visible only to the author and to the church's Pastor/Elders, who may respond. Users should not submit information in a prayer request that they are not comfortable being seen by those leaders.

## 8. Projects and fundraising

- Fundraising projects follow a defined lifecycle: proposal → Pastor approval → fundraising → in progress → completed.
- Contributions to a project are recorded by the Financial Secretary or given online, and are shown in an anonymized view to regular members.
- Only a Pastor can approve or delete a project.

## 9. Church deletion and data

- Only an Elder can delete a church workspace.
- A deleted church enters a 30-day grace period during which the deletion can be reversed by an Elder.
- After the grace period, all data — database records and stored files — is permanently and automatically purged. This action cannot be undone after the grace period ends.
